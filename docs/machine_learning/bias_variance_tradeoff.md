- Bias: The gap between predicted value and actual outcome.
- Variance: when the predicted value is scattered all over the place.

>High Bais and Low Variance: Predictions are closely but away from the actual.
>High Bias and High Variance: Predictions are wide spread but away from the actual.
>Low Bias and Low Variance: Ideal case, means prediction is closely and near the actual.

> while making the prediction we need to adjust the bias and variance of the data. this is called _Bias Variance tradeoff_

> Bias is on the neuron, not the connection.

An AI system can create simple rules in its training data that don't work well with the larger test data.

- Underfitting the data:
	- work well in home doesn't work well outside for my son.

- Overfitting the data:
	- some times we add so many complexity that becomes difficult to handle for our son.
> we need to balance it.
